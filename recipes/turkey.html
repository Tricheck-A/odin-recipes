Thanksgiving Turkey

DESCRIPTION

A perfectly seasoned and juicy turkey. My friend from France taught me to cook turkey like this,
but she never measures anything so use your own judgment for vegetable amounts, etc.
I usually err on the side of 'more is better'!

Per Serving:
594 calories; protein 69.8g; carbohydrates 1.6g; fat 31.2g; cholesterol 241.9mg; sodium 7846mg.


INGREDIENTS

  • 1 (18 pound) whole turkey, neck and giblets removed
  • 22 cups kosher salt
  • Â½ cup butter, melted
  • 2 large onions, peeled and chopped
  • 4 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup dry white wine

STEPS

  1. Step
    Rub the turkey inside and out with the kosher salt. Place the bird in a large stock pot,
    and cover with cold water. Place in the refrigerator, and allow the turkey to soak in the
    salt and water mixture 12 hours, or overnight.

  2. Step
    Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Thoroughly rinse the turkey, and
    discard the brine mixture.

  3. Step
    Brush the turkey with 1/2 the melted butter. Place breast side down on a roasting rack
    in a shallow roasting pan. Stuff the turkey cavity with 1 onion, 1/2 the carrots, 1/2 the
    celery, 1 sprig of thyme, and the bay leaf. Scatter the remaining vegetables and thyme
    around the bottom of the roasting pan, and cover with the white wine.

  4. Step
    Roast uncovered 3 1/2 to 4 hours in the preheated oven, until the internal
    temperature of the thigh reaches 180 degrees F (85 degrees C). Carefully turn the
    turkey breast side up about 2/3 through the roasting time, and brush with the
    remaining butter. Allow the bird to stand about 30 minutes before carving.
